# Intelligence Through Differences

**Summary**: Alexander Naumenko's theory that intelligence operates fundamentally through differentiation — identifying and managing differences — rather than through similarity matching. The game of 20 Questions illustrates how binary exclusion is far more efficient than comparison.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## The Theory

> "My approach to intelligence really centers on the ability to handle differences. This shifts the computation behind cognition to focus on comparisons rather than mathematical operations or calculations." — Alexander Naumenko

Traditional views of intelligence emphasize pattern recognition through similarity — matching new inputs against known patterns. Naumenko argues the opposite: intelligence excels through *differentiation*, narrowing possibilities by identifying what something is NOT.

## The 20 Questions Proof

In 20 Questions, each binary question eliminates half of remaining possibilities. With 20 questions, you can distinguish among over 1 million items. This works because:
- Each question focuses on a **difference** (is it alive? is it larger than a breadbox?)
- The process is one of systematic **exclusion**, not comparison
- It converges rapidly on the target through **narrowing**, not broadening

This is "far more effective for quick, accurate identification than comparisons based on similarities."

## Connection to DSRP

Naumenko's theory aligns with the [Dsrp Thinking](/concepts/dsrp-thinking) model — particularly the "Distinctions" element. Making distinctions (identifying differences) is foundational to all cognitive processes.

## Application to Customer Discovery

For product innovation, intelligence through differences means:
- Focus on what **distinguishes** this customer's situation from others
- Identify the **constraints** that narrow the solution space
- Ask questions that **exclude** irrelevant possibilities rather than broadly gathering similarities
- Recognize that the same object (apple) means different things (food vs. weapon) based on **contextual differences**

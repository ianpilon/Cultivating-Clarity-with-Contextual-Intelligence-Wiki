# Struggling Moments

**Summary**: The core unit of customer pain in Cultivating Clarity — the specific physical and psychological micro-interactions where customers experience friction, frustration, or inability to make progress toward their goals. Understanding these moments is the fundamental purpose of contextual intelligence in product innovation.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Definition

Struggling moments are the specific instances where customers experience difficulty, friction, or blockage in their attempts to make progress toward their goals. They are not abstract "pain points" but concrete, describable episodes that customers can articulate as experiences rather than desires.

> "True innovation in software development stems not from the speed of creation but from the depth of using contextual intelligence to gather, inform, and understand the customer's physical and psychological struggling moments that are preventing them from making progress toward their goals." (source: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md)

## Why Struggling Moments Matter

### The Demand Signal
Struggling moments are where demand lives. If a customer struggles enough with something, they become "not only willing but eager to pay for the solutions we are distinctively positioned to provide." Without a struggling moment, there is no demand — and without demand, any product is a hobby.

### The Accumulation Effect
Individual struggling moments compound over time into "unrealized goals and unfulfilling lives." A single moment of friction with a slow computer may be tolerable. Months of accumulated friction creates the emotional pressure that eventually triggers a [switching decision](/concepts/the-switching-moment).

### The Specificity Requirement
Not all struggles are relevant. "Just as a lawyer finds no value in understanding tooth pain, irrelevant to their field of practice, and a dentist is unconcerned with the intricacies of wrongful accusations, our focus must be laser-targeted." The goal is to find struggles that:
1. Are genuinely painful to the customer
2. Fall within our domain of expertise
3. Are acute enough to motivate payment

## How to Discover Struggling Moments

### What NOT to Ask
> "That's precisely why we avoid asking customers directly what they 'want,' as such responses may not deeply inform us about their aspirations or the core problems they face."

Customers can describe their problems but not design solutions. Asking "what do you want?" produces the fantasy layer of language (see [Three Layers of Language](/concepts/three-layers-of-language)). Asking about struggling moments gets to the causal layer.

### The JTBD Interview Approach
From [Bob Moesta](/interviews/bob-moesta): Focus on the [switching moment](/concepts/the-switching-moment) — when someone actually changed behavior. Investigate the timeline: What was happening when you first noticed the problem? What did you try? What didn't work? What finally made you act?

### Ethnographic Observation
From Ian's experience at Arctic Wolf Networks: observe people in their actual work context. He watched security analysts juggling five monitors, identified context-switching as the core struggling moment, and designed a single-screen solution that dramatically increased capacity.

### The NeuroKick Approach
Ian's AI leadership tool represents a new frontier: using technology to detect struggling moments in real-time. The tool listens for specific behavioral patterns (accidental diminishing behaviors) that the manager may not be consciously aware of — making invisible struggles visible.

- **[Jobs to Be Done](/concepts/jobs-to-be-done)**: The "job" is the progress being blocked by the struggling moment

## The Products-as-Interactions Insight

> "The products we create are only interactions between the moments of their lives. The only reason to ever build a product that you want to sell is if it's in service to the demand of a customer trying to make progress; everything else is a hobby."

Products don't exist in isolation — they exist as interventions within the flow of a customer's life. A product's value is measured entirely by how effectively it removes or reduces struggling moments that stand between the customer and their goals.

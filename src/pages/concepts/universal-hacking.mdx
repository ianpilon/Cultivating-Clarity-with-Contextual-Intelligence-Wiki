# Universal Hacking

**Summary**: Michael Levin's concept that all biological agents navigate the world by "hacking" — using systems in ways they weren't intended for, building internal models from their own perspective, and discovering control surfaces through experimentation. There is no manual; every agent must construct its own understanding.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## The Concept

> "Everything is trying to hack everything else, and by hacking I don't mean in the negative sense of exploitation, although that's certainly part of it, but rather, using whatever your perspective of a system is to control it, to get it to do something — to use systems how you want to, not how someone/something else meant for you to use them." — Michael Levin

The critical distinction: in biology, there is **no correct way** to use a system. There is no manual, no intended use, no designer's specification. Every agent must discover what works from its own perspective.

## "The Child as a Hacker"

Levin references a paper with this title. Children enter the world without knowing the "right way" to do anything:
- They build internal models through experimentation
- They "subvert anything you plan" because they have no allegiance to adults' categories
- They must construct their own understanding of how to "cut up the world into functional pieces"

All agents are like this. "Being an agent means you have to have your own point of view from which you develop a version of how to cut up the world into functional pieces. You don't want to, and you actually can't take anybody else's perspective on how to do it."

## Implications for Product Innovation

### Customers Are Hackers
Customers don't use products the way designers intended — they hack them to serve their own goals. Understanding *how* customers repurpose, misuse, or work around a product reveals the real [Struggling Moments](/concepts/struggling-moments) and the actual [Jobs to Be Done](/concepts/jobs-to-be-done).

### Designers Must Hack Context
Product teams are themselves agents who must construct their own understanding of the customer's world. No framework (including the [Customer Context Map](/frameworks/customer-context-map)) can substitute for the direct, exploratory work of building internal models of customer context.

### Innovation Is Hacking
Finding new interfaces, new uses, new connections between existing elements — this is what both biological agents and innovators do. [Engineered Serendipity](/concepts/engineered-serendipity) is a form of systematic hacking applied to data.

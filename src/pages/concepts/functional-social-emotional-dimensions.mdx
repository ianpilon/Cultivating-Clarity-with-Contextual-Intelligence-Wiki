# Functional, Social, and Emotional Dimensions

**Summary**: Bob Moesta's framework describing three types of energy that drive purchasing decisions — functional (mechanical), social (chemical), and emotional (electrical). These transform between states, with accumulated energy eventually triggering behavior change.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## The Three Dimensions

### Functional (Mechanical Energy)
What the product physically does or fails to do. "My computer runs slow." This is the most visible dimension — the one product teams typically focus on.

### Social (Chemical Energy)
How others perceive the choice. "My boss will think I'm incompetent if I can't get this done." Social context adds a layer of meaning that transforms functional problems into interpersonal ones.

### Emotional (Electrical Energy)
How the situation makes the person feel. "I'm afraid I'll lose my job." This is the dimension that most powerfully drives action — accumulated emotional energy is what eventually triggers the [The Switching Moment](/concepts/the-switching-moment).

## Energy Transformation

These dimensions are not separate silos but transform between states:

1. **Functional problem** → slow computer (mechanical)
2. **Social implication** → boss's negative perception (chemical)
3. **Emotional buildup** → fear of job loss (electrical)
4. **Behavior change** → buying a new computer, switching jobs, etc.

"It's like the energy transforms between these states, causing a manifestation of behavior change."

## Implications

- **Functional problems alone rarely drive change**: A slow computer is tolerable until the social and emotional consequences accumulate
- **Marketing that addresses only function misses the driver**: "Most people, especially in branding and marketing, talk about what the product is, not what it does" — and even "what it does" is only the functional layer
- **The emotional dimension is where the switch happens**: The actual trigger for action is usually emotional, not functional
- **All three must be understood**: [Customer Context Map](/frameworks/customer-context-map) research should probe all three dimensions, not just the functional

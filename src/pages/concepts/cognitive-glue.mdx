# Cognitive Glue

**Summary**: Michael Levin's term for the biological mechanisms that enable individual cells to form collectives with goals larger than any individual could pursue. While inspirational for team design, Levin warns against directly copying these principles because biology doesn't optimize for human values like individual dignity.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## The Concept

When cells combine into organisms, the collective develops goals (building organs, maintaining body temperature) far larger than individual cells could pursue (managing sugar levels). The "cognitive glue" is whatever mechanism enables this scaling of goals — bioelectric networks, chemical signaling, gap junctions, and other biological communication systems.

## The Warning

> "When you as a giant collective go spend a day rock climbing, you're going to leave a whole bunch of your skin cells on that wall, and you're going to come back and say, this was great, I met a bunch of social goals... But nobody asked those cells if they were willing to die." — Michael Levin

Biological collectives gain capabilities without tracking individual welfare. Direct application to human organizations risks reproducing the same dynamic — sacrificing individuals for collective goals. "In the political arena, that kind of forced collectivism has been tried a number of times in history and it always works the same way — human rights disasters."

## The Principle for Teams

> "We need to be looking for optimal policies for scaling collective intelligence, not necessarily copying what biology does, because I don't think biology is tracking all the values that we have (or should have)."

The lesson is not to copy biology but to be *inspired* by it while maintaining human values: individual consent, transparency, dignity ([Ori Freiman](/interviews/ori-freiman)'s ethical framework).

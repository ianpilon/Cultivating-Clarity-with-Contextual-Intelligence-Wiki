# Trade-offs vs. Compromises

**Summary**: Bob Moesta's critical distinction — a trade-off is a conscious choice to give something up in order to gain something else (satisfying), while a compromise is settling for less than expected (dissatisfying). Understanding which one a customer is making reveals fundamentally different opportunity spaces.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## The Distinction

- **Compromise**: You expect X, then settle for X minus 2. You *settle* — and inevitably feel dissatisfied. Expectations were not met.
- **Trade-off**: You consciously give something up to get something else. Expectations are *realigned*. Satisfaction comes from making an informed choice.

> "Most people think trade-offs and compromises are the same thing. They're not." — Bob Moesta

## Why It Matters for Product Development

### Compromises Signal Unmet Demand
When customers are compromising, they have expectations that aren't being met. This is a signal for innovation — build something that meets those expectations and you capture demand.

### Trade-offs Signal Priority Hierarchies
When customers make trade-offs, they reveal what they value most. The thing they're willing to give up is less important than the thing they're gaining. This reveals the hierarchy of values that should drive product design.

### Different Design Responses
- **For compromises**: Build the thing they're settling for less of — fulfill the unmet expectation
- **For trade-offs**: Understand the value hierarchy and design accordingly — don't try to eliminate the trade-off (it may be acceptable), but make sure you're on the right side of it

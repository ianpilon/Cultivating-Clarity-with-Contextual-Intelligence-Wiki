# DSRP Thinking

**Summary**: The Distinctions-Systems-Relationships-Perspectives framework from Derek Cabrera and Laura Colosi's "Thinking at Every Desk" — a systems thinking model that Ian adopted in 2020, which aligns with Alexander Naumenko's independently developed theory of intelligence through differences.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## The Four Elements

1. **Distinctions** — Identifying what something IS and IS NOT (differences, boundaries)
2. **Systems** — Recognizing parts and wholes (how elements compose larger entities)
3. **Relationships** — Seeing connections between elements (how things influence each other)
4. **Perspectives** — Taking different viewpoints (how the same thing looks from different positions)

These four elements underpin all cognitive processes and are universally applicable to information structuring.

## Connection to the Book

Ian adopted DSRP in 2020, and it became part of his mental toolkit for thinking about context. Finding [Alexander Naumenko](/interviews/alexander-naumenko)'s independently developed theory — which emphasizes Distinctions (differences) as the foundation of intelligence — was a powerful validation.

The entire book can be read through the DSRP lens:
- **Distinctions**: [Intelligence Through Differences](/concepts/intelligence-through-differences), [Trade Offs vs Compromises](/concepts/trade-offs-vs-compromises), customer vs. consumer
- **Systems**: [Mark Rubin](/interviews/mark-rubin)'s interconnected mental models, [Cognitive Light Cone](/concepts/cognitive-light-cone)'s nested goal hierarchies
- **Relationships**: [Engineered Serendipity](/concepts/engineered-serendipity), [Context Definition](/concepts/context-definition) as the things "around" something
- **Perspectives**: The nine interviews themselves, [Observer Dependent Context](/concepts/observer-dependent-context), [Robert Scoble](/interviews/robert-scoble)'s AI contextual committees

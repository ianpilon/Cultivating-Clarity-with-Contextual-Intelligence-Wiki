# Information Foraging Theory

**Summary**: The theory that humans scan their information environment much like animals forage for food — seeking maximum relevant information with minimum effort. At low context resolution, customers are information foragers making rapid, System 1 decisions based on environmental cues.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## The Theory

Information Foraging Theory proposes that humans navigate information environments using strategies analogous to animal foraging: scanning for "patches" of relevant information, evaluating the "scent" of information trails, and optimizing the ratio of valuable information gained to effort expended.

## Role in the Customer Context Map

Information Foraging operates at the **micro-level** of the [Context Resolution Gradient](/concepts/context-resolution-gradient) — where [System 1](/concepts/system-1-and-system-2-thinking) thinking dominates:

- Customers scan quickly for relevant cues
- Decisions are driven by limited attention spans and familiarity preferences
- The goal is to satisfy current needs with minimal cognitive effort
- Behaviors include clicking, scrolling, scanning headlines, and impulse actions

## Design Implications

By understanding information foraging:
- **Reduce friction** in common paths (don't make customers forage for basic functions)
- **Provide clear scent trails** (intuitive navigation, prominent calls-to-action)
- **Match information density to foraging mode** (don't overwhelm scanners with detail)
- **Create compelling patches** (landing pages, product cards, ad copy that rewards quick scanning)

## Integration with JTBD

Information foraging captures the *how* of micro-level customer behavior; [Jobs to Be Done](/concepts/jobs-to-be-done) captures the *why* at the macro level. The [Customer Context Map](/frameworks/customer-context-map) integrates both: foraging behaviors at low resolution provide entry points for discovering the deeper jobs and aspirations that drive behavior at higher resolutions.

# Implementation Guide

**Summary**: Step-by-step process for applying the Customer Contextual Intelligence framework in practice — from initial assessment through continuous iteration.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Phase 1: Assess Current State

1. **Audit existing customer understanding**: What do you actually know about customers? Is it System 1 (assumptions, surface data) or System 2 (deep contextual understanding)?
2. **Identify the gaps**: Where are you relying on assumptions vs. evidence? (See [Identifying the Gaps](/problem/identifying-the-gaps))
3. **Choose a starting point**: One customer segment, one product, one problem space

## Phase 2: Gather Contextual Intelligence

1. **Low resolution first**: Review existing behavioral data (analytics, support tickets, usage patterns)
2. **Medium resolution**: Conduct [Jobs to Be Done](/concepts/jobs-to-be-done) interviews focused on [The Switching Moment](/concepts/the-switching-moment) — talk to people who recently changed their behavior
3. **High resolution**: Explore life narratives, values, and aspirations through deep ethnographic engagement

At each level, probe for [Struggling Moments](/concepts/struggling-moments) across [functional, social, and emotional dimensions](/concepts/functional-social-emotional-dimensions).

## Phase 3: Synthesize and Map

1. **Build the [Customer Context Map](/frameworks/customer-context-map)**: Plot customer goals (Do Goals and Be Goals), journey stages, and struggling moments
2. **Identify patterns**: What recurring themes emerge across interviews?
3. **Define the jobs**: What progress are customers trying to make?
4. **Articulate the demand**: What would customers pay to have resolved?

## Phase 4: Align the Team

1. **Share findings**: Use the map as a [shared visual reference](/frameworks/context-centered-design)
2. **Align perspectives** before jumping to solutions ([Jeff Jonas](/interviews/jeff-jonas)'s three-step process)
3. **Prioritize by demand**: Focus on struggles that are genuinely painful and within your capability

## Phase 5: Design and Validate

1. **Design for outcomes, not outputs** ([Bob Moesta](/interviews/bob-moesta)'s distinction)
2. **Prototype with contextual awareness**: Test in the contexts where customers actually experience the problem
3. **Validate at the causal layer**: Don't accept pablum-level feedback — investigate [causal explanations](/concepts/three-layers-of-language)

## Phase 6: Iterate Continuously

1. **Build feedback loops**: Customer contexts evolve; your understanding must evolve with them
2. **Revisit the map**: Update as new insights emerge
3. **Expand scope**: Apply the framework to additional segments, products, or problem spaces

## Principles Throughout

- **Start with demand, not supply** ([Demand Side vs Supply Side](/concepts/demand-side-vs-supply-side))
- **Empathize before analyzing** ([Empathy in Innovation](/concepts/empathy-in-innovation))
- **Seek the causal layer** ([Three Layers of Language](/concepts/three-layers-of-language))
- **Focus on struggling moments** ([Struggling Moments](/concepts/struggling-moments))
- **Consider ethics throughout** ([Ethics and Context](/synthesis/ethics-and-context))

# Jobs to Be Done Flow Diagram

**Summary**: The visual companion to the Customer Context Map that operationalizes the JTBD framework — showing how customer discovery flows from low-resolution behavioral data through medium-resolution pattern recognition to high-resolution life narrative understanding.

## Overview

The JTBD Flow Diagram works in conjunction with the [Customer Context Map](/frameworks/customer-context-map) to provide a step-by-step visual process for customer discovery. It maps the flow of understanding from initial data points to deep insight, organized along the [Context Resolution Gradient](/concepts/context-resolution-gradient).

## The Flow

### Entry Point: Low Resolution Insights
Behavioral data (clicks, purchases, support tickets) triggers curiosity. These System 1 data points form the **starting point** of customer discovery — they show *what* is happening but not *why*.

### Investigation: Medium Resolution Patterns
[Jobs to Be Done](/concepts/jobs-to-be-done) interviews and problem-solving studies reveal the **patterns and jobs** behind the behaviors. This is where the switch from System 1 to System 2 thinking occurs — moving from observing to understanding.

Key questions at this stage:
- What progress is the customer trying to make?
- What are their [Struggling Moments](/concepts/struggling-moments)?
- What forces drive and resist change? (push, pull, habit, anxiety)
- What was the [switching moment](/concepts/the-switching-moment)?

### Deep Understanding: High Resolution Life Context
Strategic and exploratory research reveals the **life narrative** that gives the jobs meaning. This is where "Do Goals" (immediate actions) connect to "Be Goals" (identity aspirations).

### Output: Product Backlog
The understanding feeds directly into prioritization — establishing and ordering design and engineering tasks based on deep understanding of customer context. This ensures the product backlog is driven by genuine demand, not assumptions.

## Integration with Thinking Systems

| Flow Stage | Resolution | Thinking | Framework |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Data observation | Low | System 1 | Information Foraging |
| Pattern discovery | Medium | System 1→2 | JTBD interviews |
| Life narrative | High | Deep System 2 | Ethnographic research |
| Backlog priority | All | Synthesis | Customer Context Map |

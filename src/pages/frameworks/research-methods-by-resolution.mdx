# Research Methods by Resolution

**Summary**: A practical guide to matching customer research methods with the appropriate level of context resolution — from tactical optimization at low resolution to exploratory life-narrative research at high resolution.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Four Research Types

### Tactical Research (Low Resolution)
**Purpose**: Explore and authenticate potential solutions for well-defined problems
**Methods**: A/B testing, click tracking, heat maps, task completion rates, instantaneous reaction studies, recognition-based judgment tests
**Thinking mode**: System 1 — capturing automatic, instinctive reactions
**Output**: Validation or rejection of specific design decisions

### Operational Research (Low → Medium Resolution)
**Purpose**: Quantify effectiveness of existing solutions and optimize in place
**Methods**: Usability studies, analytics dashboards, conversion funnel analysis, limited attention span studies
**Thinking mode**: System 1 → 2 transition — beginning to ask "why"
**Output**: Performance metrics and optimization opportunities

### Strategic Research (Medium → High Resolution)
**Purpose**: Identify new opportunities and unaddressed needs that could steer future initiatives
**Methods**: Customer interviews, problem-solving studies, adaptability research, informed choice experiments, [Jobs to Be Done](/concepts/jobs-to-be-done) timeline investigations
**Thinking mode**: System 2 — deliberate, reflective, goal-oriented inquiry
**Output**: Customer journey maps, job definitions, opportunity landscapes

### Exploratory Research (High Resolution)
**Purpose**: Venture into new markets and investigate potential areas for significant future revenue
**Methods**: Strategic planning studies, values-driven decision experiments, life transition research, deep ethnographic engagement
**Thinking mode**: Deep System 2 — integrative, narrative-constructing, identity-level understanding
**Output**: Customer life narratives, value hierarchies, "Be Goal" definitions

## Iterative Flow

These research types are not sequential but iterative:
- Start with tactical to identify interesting patterns
- Move to operational to quantify
- Shift to strategic to understand the "why"
- Venture into exploratory to discover new territory
- Loop back to tactical to validate new hypotheses

# Context-Centered Design

**Summary**: The approach to product and team design that places shared contextual understanding at the center of decision-making — evolved through Ian's conversations with Jeff Jonas and Bob Moesta, and operationalized through the Customer Context Map.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Origin

Ian developed the idea of a "context-centered thinking canvas" while struggling with team alignment challenges. He showed early sketches to [Jeff Jonas](/interviews/jeff-jonas) during their interview, seeking feedback on a framework that could help teams find common ground faster.

## Jonas's Refinement

Jonas immediately reframed the concept:
- Rejected "whole truth" at the center → replaced with "aligned perspective"
- Proposed the observation space as the starting point (what are everyone's observations?)
- Outlined the progression: align perspectives → align outcomes → align measures
- Warned against getting to KPIs too early
- Compared it to the James Webb telescope mirror alignment

## Moesta's Contribution

Bob added the demand-side lens: the center of any design conversation should be the customer's [Struggling Moments](/concepts/struggling-moments), not the team's opinions. "It's okay to want to build all the shiny things, but we need to see if people actually want those things."

## The Framework

Context-centered design asks teams to:
1. Explicitly state their individual perspectives (product, design, engineering, business)
2. Share observations about the customer's context
3. Find alignment on customer outcomes before jumping to solutions
4. Use the [Customer Context Map](/frameworks/customer-context-map) as a shared visual reference
5. Debate productively about trade-offs, not opinions

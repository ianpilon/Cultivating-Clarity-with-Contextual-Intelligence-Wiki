## The Four Gaps

### 1. Lack of Confidence in Creating Valuable Products
Teams have low confidence that customers will perceive value in their innovations. The themes of simplifying complex concepts through analogies and understanding emotional context directly address this — products must be made accessible and emotionally resonant, ensuring customers not only comprehend but *feel* the value.

### 2. Misalignment Between Development Efforts and Customer Needs
The emphasis on [Empathy in Innovation](/concepts/empathy-in-innovation), understanding customer motivations through [Jobs to Be Done](/concepts/jobs-to-be-done), and leveraging [Engineered Serendipity](/concepts/engineered-serendipity) speaks to the alignment gap. True innovation comes from aligning product development with actual, often unarticulated customer needs.

### 3. Over-reliance on Logical Reasoning Without Customer Input
Logical reasoning alone is insufficient. It must be complemented with [emotional considerations](/concepts/affective-context), ethical and societal contexts ([Ethics and Context](/synthesis/ethics-and-context)), and appreciation for the fluid nature of [Struggling Moments](/concepts/struggling-moments). Products must be "not only logically sound but also deeply connected to the human experience."

### 4. Balancing Technology/Vision Focus with Customer-Centricity
The frustration of balancing visionary ambitions with customer-centricity. [AI and technology](/synthesis/ai-and-contextual-intelligence) must be leveraged responsibly while remaining "deeply rooted in a profound understanding that it's humans at the end of the day that ultimately use AI as yet another tool of satisfying their needs."

## Industry-Specific Manifestations

- **Healthcare apps**: Fail to account for diverse patient populations' unique needs
- **Educational platforms**: Neglect emotional and social factors impacting learning
- **Enterprise software**: Technically advanced but fails to integrate with existing workflows and cultural norms
- **Social media**: Prioritizes growth over mental health and well-being
- **Recommendation algorithms**: Reinforce biases and misinformation

## The Good News

Proven strategies exist: JTBD methodology, empathy-driven research, context-resolution frameworks, and the [Customer Context Map](/frameworks/customer-context-map). The gap is not in available tools but in organizational willingness to invest in upfront customer understanding.

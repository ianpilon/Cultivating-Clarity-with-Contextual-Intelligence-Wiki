# Ethics and Context

**Summary**: How context shapes and is shaped by ethical considerations in technology development — spanning Ori Freiman's philosophical framework, Nick Shackleton-Jones's machine ethics argument, Amjad Hassoun's data bias insights, and Michael Levin's warning about collective intelligence values.

**Sources**: CULTIVATING CLARITY.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

## Core Thesis

> "Context is a very important factor in influencing meaning, but also interpreting meaning of anything that has to do with ethics, moral values, and with the principles we adopt." — Ori Freiman

Ethics is not a fixed set of rules but a context-dependent framework that shifts with culture, technology, time, and situation.

## Four Ethical Dimensions from the Interviews

### 1. Ethics as Context-Dependent Meaning (Ori Freiman)
Traditional ethical frameworks (utilitarianism, Kantianism) are insufficient for the digital age. Modern ethical concerns center on social justice, concentration of power, individual autonomy, and data rights. [Ori Freiman](/interviews/ori-freiman) argues for absolute principles (human dignity) while acknowledging that their *application* is context-dependent.

Practical framework for AI tools: consent, purpose, data ownership, right to erasure, transparency.

### 2. Machines Cannot Be Ethical (Nick Shackleton-Jones)
Because ethics is based on *feeling* (see [Affective Context](/concepts/affective-context)), machines without genuine emotional capacity cannot be truly ethical. "It is simply impossible to have a machine that can be ethical in the same way that you and I do." Current AI produces simulations of ethical behavior, not the thing itself.

### 3. AI Reveals Human Biases (Amjad Hassoun)
AI trained on biased data perpetuates bias — but it also *reveals* bias that was previously invisible. "The only way to reveal our human flaws is by training an AI model on it." However, "cleaning" training data to remove bias introduces a different bias — hiding history rather than addressing root causes.

### 4. Biological Collectives vs. Human Values (Michael Levin)
Biology's [Cognitive Glue](/concepts/cognitive-glue) scales collective intelligence without tracking individual welfare. Directly copying this for human organizations risks "human rights disasters." Human ethical frameworks must be applied to any collective intelligence design, even when inspired by biological patterns.

## The Disinformation Case

Ori's example of organized foreign disinformation campaigns illustrates where absolute ethics must apply: "By the time we realize it was a foreign campaign, we've already lost each other; we're not speaking the same language." Some actions are "absolutely wrong" regardless of context — but recognizing them requires contextual awareness to detect.

## For Product Teams

1. Consider the ethical context in which your product operates — not just functionality
2. Build transparency and consent into data-collecting features
3. Be aware of bias in training data and recommendation algorithms
4. Don't assume your values match your customers' values
5. Design for human dignity as a non-negotiable requirement

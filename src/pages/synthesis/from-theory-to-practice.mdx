## Ian's Personal Transformation

The book documents Ian's shift from design-thinking + rapid-prototyping as his primary compass to a deeper understanding of [Affective Context](/concepts/affective-context) and customer struggling moments:

> "I used to think that logic, creativity, and vision alone could create the next big thing. Then I went on a journey to learn about 'context' from different perspectives... and now I think that affective context and a true understanding of how and why customers struggle has an even stronger influence on the probability of whether or not a product will actually get used."

This transformation — from "What do users want?" to "What do people dream of achieving, and why? What struggle lies in the way?" — is the book's central narrative arc.

## The FitLife Example

A hypothetical fitness app illustrating the framework in action:

**Discovery**: Users were struggling with onboarding and dropping off before establishing workout routines.

**Contextual Analysis**: Understanding the context — new users' anxiety about starting, information overload from too many options, lack of personalized guidance, absence of progress visibility.

**Solution**: Refined onboarding with personalized recommendations and progress tracking features.

**Result**: Significant increase in user engagement and retention.

This example demonstrates the full cycle: identifying [Struggling Moments](/concepts/struggling-moments), understanding the context at multiple resolutions, and designing a solution that addresses the real problem.

## Implementation Principles

1. **Start Small** — Begin with a specific customer segment or single product
2. **Iterate** — Use an iterative approach to continuously refine understanding
3. **Scale** — Expand to other segments once comfortable with the framework
4. **Communicate** — Share findings and progress with the team
5. **Adapt** — Be ready to adjust based on new insights

## The Ongoing Commitment

> "Adopting this framework is not a one-time event, but an ongoing journey of learning and adaptation. As customer needs and contexts evolve, so too must our understanding and our solutions."
